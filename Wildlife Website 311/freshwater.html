OVERVIEW

Malaysia receives an average of 3,000 mm annually of ample rainfall, leading to an approximate annual water resource of some 900 billion cubic metres. Approximately 97 percent of our availability of raw water for agricultural, domestic and industrial uses is extracted mainly from rivers. There are 189 river basins in Malaysia - 89 in Peninsular Malaysia, 78 in Sabah and 22 in Sarawak. From the highlands, all the rivers source and flood.






CONSERVING RIVER BASINS

Rivers also contribute to our lives in many ways, in addition to being a water supply. In reality, as they shape us spiritually, socially and economically, we are very closely entangled with rivers. For our food supplies, we rely on rivers, and the livelihoods of riverine populations in particular are substantially dependent on rivers. We use rivers as transport and navigation channels, hydroelectric power generation, irrigation of agricultural land, as centres for new settlements and recreation.






SUSTAINABLE WATER USE

All of us agree that water is needed in all facets of our lives and that our well-being requires sufficient supply of high quality water. Growth will still not be feasible without water - it's a common aspect that cuts through all development industries. We are fortunate in Malaysia with an abundance of rainfall and water supplies. It has been estimated that we each enjoy more than 5,000 m3 of renewable water per capita per person per year.






CONSERVING FRESHWATER HABITATS

The highland forests and wetlands (both forested wetlands and water bodies such as rivers and lakes) are significant freshwater ecosystems in the country. Forests in the highlands, also referred to as natural 'water towers' because of their role of water catchment, provide us provide us with a continuous safe water supply. They are the source of much of the water supplies of the country.




Numerous threats and problems are facing these freshwater environments. Recognizing this, Malaysian Wildlife supports the protection of freshwater habitats, adaptive maintenance and sustainable use.
