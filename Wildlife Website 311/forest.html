THE MALAYSIAN RAINFOREST

Tropical rainforests support the largest diversity of living species on Earth among all forms of forests. Rain forests are the undisputed champions of biodiversity in the world's habitats, with a total population of 3 to 50 million species of flora and fauna. Southeast Asia's rainforests, like Malaysia, are known to be the world's oldest and one of the most biologically rich. This rainforest richness is not accidental, but the product of a sequence of unusual circumstances.






MAJOR CONCERN

The surface of Malaysia's land was once almost completely surrounded by forest. Forests still occupy about 54 percent of the overall surface area today. Deforestation is, however, a big concern as the country is still growing rapidly. There was a loss of around 8.12 million hectares of land cover in Malaysia from 2001 to 2019. This is equal to a drop in tree cover of 28 percent since 2000. In addition to deforestation, the remaining forests face the danger of unregulated harvesting, the illicit destruction of forest resources and the invasion of agriculture and urbanisation.






PROTECTED AREAS FOR THRIVING ECOSYSTEMS

For the protection of natural ecosystems, species and genetic diversity, protected areas are important. There is a great deal of opportunity in Malaysia to create more protected areas and to develop existing ones. Safe areas have also been referred to historically as the core of biodiversity. The gross forested area of the world in 2011 is estimated to be 17.98 million hectares, according to the forestry departments of Peninsular Malaysia, Sabah and Sarawak (MTIB, 2012).






SAVING FORESTS, SAVING OURSELVES

Borneo conjures up visions of adventure, alien animals and experiences with varied people on a magical island. For conservationists, Borneo is one of the hotspots for biodiversity in the world. Heart of Borneo, an innovative restoration scheme that others may have heard of, covers not only any region in Borneo, but highland forests at the heart or centre of the island.






HEART OF BORNEO

Borneo is one of the world's oldest hotspots of rainforests and habitats. A 22 million hectare landscape of forests is covered by the Heart of Borneo (HoB) corridor, home to a diverse group of wildlife species such as proboscis monkey, clouded leopard, orangutan and Bornean elephant. It is also one of the global priority protected areas of the Malaysian Wildlife and an important region for local and indigenous communities in socio-economic development. It is necessary to ensure well defined forest conservation and sustainable land use in order to maintain a harmonious relationship between people and nature.






SABAH LANDSCAPES PROGRAMME

Via our Sabah Landscapes Programme (SLP), which has three pillars: Preserve, Produce, and Rebuild, Malaysian Wildlife promotes the execution of national and state policies. By combining the conservation of trees, biodiversity and rivers with RSPO accredited palm oil processing and preservation of natural corridors and riparian reserves, SLP seeks to promote the achievement of sustainable development objectives. Malaysian Wildlife will focus on three target ecosystems across the project, such as Tabin, Tawau and Lower Sugut. Subsequent restoration efforts in the Central Forest, Kalabakan and Ulu Padas-Nabawan landscapes will also continue.




The goal of Malaysian Wildlife is to increase the coverage of forest protected areas, improve the management of production forests for sustainable timber supply, and restore degraded areas, particularly where essential forest linkages need to be preserved.
